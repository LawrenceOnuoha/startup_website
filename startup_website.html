Dr. Celeste Teal



Professor, Linguistics and Anthropology. Bashville Leadership Chair in Language Conservation. Fellow, Runes, and Languages of Wakanda (FDRLW).

Welcome to my webpage! I am Dr. Celeste Teal, a passionate and dedicated Linguistic Professor specializing in endangered, and dead languages, as well as ancient runes.

With over 12 years of experience in the field, I have conducted extensive research and published numerous articles on topics such as The Evolution of English as we Know It. My goal is to explore the intricate dynamics of language, culture, and society, and share my knowledge with students and fellow researchers.

As an educator, I strive to create an engaging and inclusive learning environment where students can develop a deep understanding of linguistic concepts and their real-world applications. I teach courses such as Ancient Aramaic, and mentor students in their research projects.

I actively participate in academic conferences and collaborate with colleagues worldwide to foster intellectual growth and contribute to the advancement of linguistic studies. I believe in the power of interdisciplinary research and enjoy exploring connections between linguistics and other fields.
